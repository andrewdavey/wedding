Wedding Day Schedule (Saturday 18th)

12:00 Arrival at Heartlands

Heartlands is a visitor attraction and World Heritage Site in Cornwall, built around an old Engine House. We're getting married in the Film Room, a converted space inside one of the old mining buildings. It's part of the exhibition rooms, next to the visitor centre.

Heartlands

Parking: Heartlands is free to enter and we're pretty sure parking is free in October.

12.30 Ceremony

Handsome groomsmen will be on hand to help you find the room and choose a seat, but please sit wherever you like, on either side of the aisle. As the saying goes, 'choose a seat, not a side'! The fantabulous Nathan Barrow is our photography guy during the ceremony, but feel free to take lots of pictures or videos for us the rest of the day.

Sorry, no confetti allowed at Heartlands.

13:00 Pasties and Pics

Enjoy a Cornish pasty, some saffron cake and a drink on us at Heartland's Red River bar and conservatory. We'll be taking a couple of pics around Heartlands, weather permitting!

15:30 Champagne Reception at Landithy Hall

Opened in 1909, Landithy Hall is at the heart of the lovely village of Madron, just outside Penzance.

Parking: is very limited! We advise a short a taxi ride from Penzance.

A glass of bubbly and a homemade chocolate truffle awaits you from 3.30pm onwards, but come whenever you're ready if you want to explore Heartlands, check into your hotel, or just take a break ☺ At Heartlands the Gunpower Gateway, Totem Circle and Diaspora gardens are worth a look; there's a great adventure playground for the kids; and Engine House tours are at 2pm (Adults £3, Concessions £1.50).

The traditional first "dance" (we can't dance) and an eclectic mix of funky music from The Choons. Tunes from the 60s to the 00s - bring your dancing shoes!
